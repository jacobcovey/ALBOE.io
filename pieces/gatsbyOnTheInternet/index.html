Message

When I was assigned to read The Great Gatsby, as a high school student, I figured I would just read a summary online (I have great regret for not reading many of the classics that were assigned to me in public school). Maybe there were a couple chapters teenage me skipped (wouldn’t put it by the bastard), but I remember being enthralled by the book and the characters. It was an important step for me realizing how much I undervalued classic novels, and literature in general.

In late 2021 I decided a book in the public domain would be the perfect project for a native HTML CSS JS project. For several reasons The Great Gatsby was the obvious choice, but in the end I picked it because I was excited to read it again. It didn’t disappoint.

Gatsby on The Internet is a single .html file containing all the templating (HTML) styling (CSS) and scripting (Javascript) required for a complete web-book experience including automatic place keeping, passage search, light/dark themes, adjustable text size, and more. It's responsive and designed to look good on all screen sizes. The title and author name graphics are embedded SVG’s I made to match the typography on the first edition cover. Everything is native HTML/CSS/JS no third party frameworks or libraries. The file size comes in at less than .5 MB (a plain .mobi version of the book comes in at .64 MB).

In a way I consider this is my love letter to the modern web tools/technologies. Thanks to all the hard working humans involved in the excruciating process of creating and updating web standards. Also thanks to The Gutenberg Project. I started project with their plain HTML version of the Great Gatsby. They have over 60,000 public domain books they share on their website gutenberg.org.

I’m writing this after just finishing the beta version. I plan to re-read the entire book again on GatsbyOnTheInternet.com as a final QA pass. I also will spend time refactoring the code. Once it’s deemed “complete” I plan on minting it as NFT. More on that later.

-Jacob
